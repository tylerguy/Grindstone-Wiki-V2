Degradation: Lead and Decay

Picking up from where we left off.

Interference is not the only means of degradation. In addition to this a construct can degrade quickly through interaction with certain materials, the most destructive of which is lead. Lead seems to soak up aether like a sponge and not like letting go so when run across a program in free space it can distort or even delete the program where it was run through altogether. The destruction of the material will not necessarily destroy programming but depending on the programming if the construct is hit forcefully a contingent spell of thermokinetics might over compensate to allow it to continue to stand causing a fast and aggressive casting within the frame and speed up degradation in the relevant lines faster. While giving a few wacks to a full frame made of iron won't necessarily cause it to shut down, constant blunt force trauma to a full frame made for defensive purposes over a long period of time and with great force might eventually leave part of the full frame's material body to become immobile if the body itself hasn't sustained enough damage to not operate alone by then.

In necromantic programming degradation of the frames are not the only concerns you should have. The body also will decay and with the material body decaying it may lose connection at points with its programming or expose the programming to be deleted. Necromancers are often programming experts who have either connections or knowledge of how to preserve dead tissue. They are able to make corrections in programming easier than in metallic bodies however as they can simply cut into the tissue and administer led to areas that require deletion. Sometimes however new programming, especially that of thermokinetics for motility, might be necessary as the body degrades. It is for this reason a programmer will have to do much more maintenance on necromantic constructs than full frames once created. In theory and with unlimited time however piece by piece programming can be added and the old body removed until the necromantic construct is indistinguishable from a full frame.

Contingency Casting

So many of you I'm sure want to get your construct up and running as fast as possible, unfortunately you'll need to learn to cast first. You should however know what you will be trying to do once you understand the basics of casting however. Lets look at the very basics and work our way up in structure starting with contingency casting and what it is.

As you know you aeonize, summate, and then give instructions to (program) a spell. To do this you utilize an aeonized object when in the weak area created by a four point. The aetherial matrix is fluid however and casting is very specific so summation has to change quickly with the understanding of how much aether is coming through the point of aeonization and if the rate may increase or decrease in the future. How do we predict the aetherial waves in the aetherial matrix? How do we calculate how much aether and at what rate is necessary for a casting? How do we account for time dilation in the 5-dimensional reality of the aetherial matrix? How do we adjust casting over time and in change of placement to the targeted area of a spell for a construct to function? To all but the latter most question I say to you, don't worry about that as another lecturer will talk about that with you.

The math gets heavy but there is something called the Motian (Mah-tee-uhn) Conjecture. This is a mathematical observance that doesn't have adequate proof but is used as a general rule of thumb when dealing with casting spells over long durations. To continue the casting however we need to conduct a special type of casting, the contingency casting.

Contingency casting is series casting, that is it is technically several spells linked together with small variable sections. The variable sections are altered depending on need or intention. You might recognize something similar in an earlier lecture and for good reason. Essentially a programmer needs to have a spell that aeonizes summates with regularity indicate a need for constant casting on top of casting according to select variables.

By ordering contingency casting you can create a simple program, a spell that casts spells effectively. Number hundreds of these together and in space they form a line. Under the assistance of aeonization magic this line is the aetherial frame of a construct. The Motian Conjecture is useless alone for casting over time. You also need to account for several other principals in casting to determine the placement of these complex contingency castings or simple programs. This is where I recommend you look back on an earlier lecture by Pauchik.
