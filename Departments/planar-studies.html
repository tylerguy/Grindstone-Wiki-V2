Plane Touch

  • A seemingly random and dangerous effect of planar travel.
    • Best described as a disconnect from the nervous system.
      • “Forgets” how to breathe.
      • Paralysis.
    • Can occur with any degree of planar travel, even a little.
      • There is a researcher in Planar Studies that deals with minimal percent of planar character (refer to this lecture) required to cause plane touch[1].
  • Classes of Plane Touch:
    • Class 1: The condition is present but very difficult to determine.
    • Class 2: Outwardly apparent and no need for alchemical treatment, but the afflicted may continue their life uninhibited.
    • Class 3: Alchemical treatment is required for the afflicted to function as though they were not afflicted.
    • Class 4: Dependency on others heavily required if they go without alchemical treatment, life still heavily affected even with treatment. Can suddenly or slowly degrade into Class 5 and is considered terminal.
    • Class 5: Lethal without treatment, dependency needed even with treatment.

Planar Overlay and Travel

“That's understandable, so you can use the atherial matrix to target which plane you want for research on the Belthidain laws? Without the use of an overlay?” - TheAutumnWriter [They/Them]

“Correct. By massive summation we can pull the aether away from space between two planes forcing this overlay. There are limitations and dangers however.” - Researcher Zuchterin

When one plane has their characteristics make up 100% of the overlapping space (refer to this lecture), it's said one within that overlapping space has travelled to that plane, if they originated from the other plane in the overlap.
    • Now planes are defined as vacated regions of Aether, so if two planes overlay, how is it that overlay happens in a select space instead of the entire plane? How do the characteristics not “fill” the other plane?

  • Terminology of the quote:

    • Synthetic aetherial exhaust: Aetherial exhaust with the interference of a caster.
