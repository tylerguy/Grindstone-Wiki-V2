The Wheel

There are more than a dozen proposed models for different situations in alchemical experimentation and it’s believed the examination of the elementary is not always practical, leading to the use of what Alchemy calls “The Wheel.” Though imperfect, it is used for general consideration and best guess in practical alchemy. It can be best described as eight segments, each representative of an alchemical property, with opposing properties across from each other.

According to the wheel, there are eight alchemical properties.

  • Fire
  • Earth
  • Air
  • Water
  • Life
  • Death
  • Abberant
  • Power

The properties align with observed natural phenomena and concepts based in religious studies. The observation is Toristic and empirical, but the logic often follows a form of symbolic logic, with results having reasonable accuracy. 

The Wheel is also helpful in aiding the measure of another characteristic called Volatility. The spaces between alchemical properties suggest higher volatility when combined and “how much” can be assumed by measuring the distance and quality of the opposition properties. However, the wheel does not verify adhesion, which is described in a different model.

Recently, there has been a proposed model that suggests 23 separate alchemical characteristics with a very complex interaction chart. It better explains some experimental results but has failed in some predictions made by the Wheel.
