The Necromancy Department contrary to the understanding most have is not limited to the animation of dead tissue. While service undead are produced by employees here the research team is dedicated to the advanced study of Aetherial programming. They understand in collaboration with other departments how to create immaterial and Aetherial systems that interact with material. Constructs of glass, metal, wood, or any real material are programmed by designing complicated multidimensional programs to cover an area called an Aetherial frame. Aside from constructs and animation the necromancy department is responsible for stationary constructs often referred to as enchanted objects. The Necromancy department assists in security, locating and correcting issues within programming, and the theory of programming the aforementioned constructs. - Blaise

Frames

  • Directionality, Rate and Intensity are the three characteristics of frames.
    • They are all important for Frames, but makes programming possibilities almost endless.

Directionality

  • Complicated, not well understand but well enough to create programs
  • Linked to the “Up-Theory”
  • Generally important to determine spatial direction of the terminus for each segment to function properly.

Rate

  • The amount of pulses over a certain time over properly activated
  • Resting Rate
    • Pulse at anytime when a particular stimuli is not in effect
  • Active Rate
    • Pulse at anytime when a particular stimuli IS in effect
  • For instance: a construct responds to a command word, when the word is spoken, the active rate is determined. Otherwise, simply resting rate.
  • Constructs require and constant and steady stream of aether, to keep a frame intact there must be a constant rate.

Intensity

  • The amount of aether within each pulse

Segments

  • there is a command pulse for the interaction of the segments of a frame (unlike a river, faucet or any other analogy using water to compare the flow of aether)
  • Segments are portions of a line that have a terminus, sometimes two, which the aetherial pulse travels through and obeys certain simple actions.
    • Terminus - a final point in space or time; an end or extremity.
    • Some lines within the frame could have dozens, hundreds, or in extreme cases thousands of segments to properly program.
    • A Large number of segments
      • Many of the segments might have several directions of facing terminus’
      • Creates a visual that upon close inspection, lines begin to look like a bunch of little lines cluttered and intersecting
