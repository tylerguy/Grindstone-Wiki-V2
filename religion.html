Nine Days to Anise

  • From The Scoundrel sect of the faith of Frik, but quite popular to scholars all over.

Nine Days to Anise is about a tax collector traveling south towards Anise after enjoying a wedding celebrating in the town of Cider. As he's leaving, he meets another traveler and they resume the journey together. As they make their way south, among conversation the traveler offers a new lesson to the taxman.

Upon arriving at Anise, the traveler gives the Tenth Lesson, the taxman pausing to consider it. When looks up, the traveler is gone and so is any proof of him being there at all. The taxman runs into town to tell people what happened, a scribe writing the lessons and creating Nine Days to Anise.

“For nine days we've traveled

Nine answers I’ve given
To nine questions you’ve posed

From the price of salvation
To the joys of charity
You've sought out my wisdom

Each man makes his own fate
His own knuckles he casts

I gave each of you freedom
I taught each of you Truth
To throw off your shackles
Your own purpose to sooth

Such was my first gift 
And so it still stands
To break the Old Rote [Roht?]
Free from Gods' commands

Then you all found new bonds
Some to wealth, some to kings
Some to the pursuit of Reason
Measure, and Order of things

If such be your pleasure
Then let it bring you glee
As you serve yourselves more

So go break the old Temples!

Embrace the new age

Know my love shall endure
