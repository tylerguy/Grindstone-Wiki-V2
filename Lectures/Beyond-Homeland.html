Introduction

Thank you all for coming to my lecture. I will be filling in for Bruno. For those simply reading this later I hope you still find it insightful. Today I will be discussing basic geography of what many people in Northern Belthas colloquially refer to as Homeland. Please note that this large land mass is not the only one known of within our world.

Homeland

Belthas is one of the largest countries within this continent located on the east coast. The land Belthas occupies is separated into several "belts" describing basic geographic characteristics but beyond this there are several large regions that were once ruled over by the noble class prior to the dissolution of the old systems of government. We will however leave the discussion of the regions for a later date and focus on the belts prior to moving to other countries.

The Southern most areas of Belthas occupy a tropical region which is where much of our sugar crop is grown. To the far South East a peninsula projects and cuffs slightly upward. It is on this cuff Seawell, a city some of you might be familiar with, is located. A chain of islands finds itself here but there are three major islands off to the North of this area. Moving West in the tropical region however a boarder is made by the Blightlands that is maintained by defensive forces on the side of Belthas mostly established by a combination of boarder patrol stationed there from the Belthean government and those stationed by various religious orders. This boarder expands to one of our great lakes and eventually into a belt known as the grain belt.

The grain belt is where much of our foodstuffs come from as well as a small region within it dedicated to much of our honey and mead production. To the East is the Black Marsh before eventually connecting with the Ocean and to the right is a boarder that partly is created by the Northern Blightlands and part by the mountains and Oudstian Boarder.

It is within this Belt and continuing into the Wetland Belt, a place most comparable to coastal North Western Europe in your world, that three more of our great lakes can be found and off the coast to the East the first few major Belthean Islands.

The Wetlands are generally referred to as such because of the rainfall found in much of this region. It is the wetlands where the Belthean Capital can be found as well as the metropolis of Paint and the defensive metropolis Dragon's Den. Dial is further north in a region more comparable to Northern United States or Southern Canada. This region is much more hilly and much cooler than the wetlands. It should be noted that there are nearly 700 recognized "major" settlements within the continent with countless other smaller farming communities and that places like Dial and Paint are given special mention due to their impact on our nation and others as epicenters of trade, defense, or culture.

Another major peninsula can be found in the far North East of Belthas that also hooks northward. This is the edge of non-contested territory for us as to the North West we enter a bitter cold region that ends at a boarder contested by the Northern Nations. A conglomeration of micronations and others that their recognition is often in question by some of the more established countries of our world. None the less, it is not part of Belthas and the boarder respected.

The North and the West

To the North of Belthas and covering the Northern edge of the continent is a collection of various nations and micronations. Given it's massive size we generally just call it "The North". To the North East of Belthas there is a place known as the eye, a weird of woodland surrounded by a large clearing. This is a bitter cold place and much of the surrounding area leading all the way to the boarder of Belthas is occupied by the Red Orcs, green skinned Orcs with a xenophobic disposition who are highly territorial. While it is uncommon for a culture or nation to be a single species these people are unique. Many attempts at contact have ended poorly and they have regularly aggressed upon the Belthian Boarder. Much of what we know about them comes from contact with their neighbors. Somewhere slightly North West of Belthas lies one of only a few major cities in the North where the higher governing body to many city states, mobile nations, and complex trade networks resides. The predominant faiths of the region are Junt and Au'Pak.

While trade can be conducted by the loop 3 of the four seasons in the year few from Belthas or Victoria attempt trade directly through the North due to various dangers including weather, terrain, the natural fauna, and the poorly understood peoples that inhabit the land. The Liath are native to the region, Gillie Mason who you have met is one such person. The Liath people live a very difficult life contending with the inhospitable nature of the North and even go without naming their children for many years until they are certain they will survive. Even with the bitter cold in the north there are two regions of what are called the bridge islands that are navigated through for the loop, the west being two and east being three such islands, that are south of what seems to be a sheet of white with hard winds and little vegetation. This large landmass is approximately half the the size of the nation of Oudste.

Further West is a place dominated by the Northen Rau and most nations, not currently including Belthas, have accepted it as an independent nation. The ability to govern some of the people on the outskirts of the established boarders however has led many to be hesitant to accept it as such yet. One such major city is a cultural and academic epicenter known is Obelus who does not claim to be governed by the coastal metropolis that is the capital named Okios of this North West nation.

Currently between one of the North West great lakes and the mountains lies a profitable region for the fur trade. This is a place currently seeing blood shed as Victoria engages in skirmishes during what we collectively call the fur war.

There are many other regions to cover and so we will need a future continuation of this lecture.
