Discovery Casting

Discovery casting was a period where some of the most important concepts and techniques in aetherial casting began to develop such as early ritual casting. The major event that scholars agree that marks the beginning of Discovery casting was the implementation of the Toristic Process. While casting remained still very spiritual in many ways the Toristic process allowed aetherial casting to become more of a study. Through the short period between the creation of this process and the eventual death of Tor he and his students were responsible for initiating an explosion of discoveries allowing aetherial magic to propel itself with practical applications. One markedly unique quality of Discovery Era Aetherial casting is that it did not rely on any practical or small objects at first. As mentioned before it would not be until the classical era that objects and four-points would be utilized as we think of them now.

Discovery casting was the shortest lived period of casting but was very important to continued cultural advancement for many peoples. It assisted in the recovery of a dark era after the fall of the Azalt which occurred not long after the death of Tor. This dark era lasted only about two generations and after rapid discovery led to advancement and opportunities in colonization. Discovery Casting in it's earliest times was considered "not real casting" as it employed more of an empirical approach and there were questions as to if results could actually be known or simply experienced. This allowed the Emperor at the time to suggest it was unnecessary for it to be regulated by the Order of the Magus. There would however be fear fostered in the dark era to lead to aetherial casting to come mostly under control of the Order of the Magus in the future, especially the East.

While casters still utilized the now impractical large areas for casting or Calculi Spaces it allowed them to realize patterns or lack there of with specific geometry of casting. Another realization made was it was more likely to draw more aether if more points of aeonization were made. This was of course before the realization of summation but allowed for early principles of foci, rituals, and exhaust. A singular specialist would often be trained to take on large amounts of aether for only programming into the desired spell. These casters would often not handle the aeonization themselves and would be masters of arithmetic and geometry. They often relied on skills they learned over time keeping the innate casting concept of aetherial casting being also an art. These were the first foci in ritual casting and the others were referred to as "contributors".

Soon new tools for calculations were created that could be handled without the need of manipulation by limbs. Several personal made items such as one discovered to utilize a series of strings so that limited movement was made during the spellcasting but the instruments could assist with calculation. This was until advent of "assisted writing".

Aether seemed to these people as being able to occupy space already occupied by other material. If one could see it they might be able to create shortcuts in casting but also use their general placement to effect the movement of aether with aether already in space. Soon specialists would begin filling small objects with aether, hyper aeonizing them and placing them in patterns to assist with the further movement of aether during large casting. These objects would soon shrink as their understanding allowed for more and more precise instruments. This ultimately lead to both the invention of programming and the early written spells.

The oldest student of Tor would take programming much further with the blessing of the Azalt Emperor.
