Introduction

Today we will be discussing Transmutation: The Intersection of Aetherial Studies and Alchemy. Before we continue to the lecture I would like to revisit a few important concepts and differentiate between them as to avoid possible confusion. Belthidian Laws are those universal laws that are constant in any plane or region of space within the 5 spatial dimensions while Natural Laws describe physical laws observed within space typically within a defined area or plane. Belthidian Laws explain Natural Laws. It is important to remember that existing models in Alchemy are imperfect and often contradict other models but are practical in explaining certain phenomena. Lastly synthesis magic is the youngest school of aetherial casting accepted by UMAD.

First Section - What is Transmutation

Transmutation to put it simply is the utilization of aetherial casting, efferent or innate, with concepts of alchemical theory to alter the material of one observed object to act as or become another. It should be recognized that transmutation is not simply limited to turning one thing into another and that having one material act like another is just as, if not more, important to the study and the practical applications of transmutation. Transmutation is also the study that fathered a further separation in aetherial studies into the dedicated school of synthesis magic. Beyond its link from Alchemy to Aetherial Studies transmutation is also important in Infernology, Divination, Theology, and many other studies.

The two most important schools of magic that led to the current studies of transmutation and prior to the development of synthesis were thermokinetics and programming. Some of the first skills one develops in transmutation are the use of thermokinetics to limit physical interactions between materials. This means we might use thermokinetics to create an "aetherial barrier" between an object and another or create a temporary container. This concept for advanced students is later applied to smaller and smaller samples of the material to inhibit or speed up the rate of anticipated reactions. This is also very important if an alchemist intends to create isolates like alchemical kindness or alchemist dust but wants the purity or grade to improve. The process between turning a higher grade from a lower might be very volatile and therefore the reaction must be conducted in a synthetic environment created by this thermokenetic manipulation.

The creation of frames is often important to creating specialized tools for particular reactions of study within transmutation and is why programming is such an important field for the early development of laboratory skills in transmutation. To better develop these aetherial mechanisms for assisted alchemy an individual should also be well equipped with an understanding of multiple studies. It is for this reason transmutation alchemists are typically scholars of advanced alchemical theory and work to discover alchemical constants to better understand the rules and models of alchemy and where they break down. Since "power" as an alchemical concept is typically attributed to contact with aether all experimentation in transmutation assumes a certain degree of artifacts in resulting data. General rules have been created to account for this.

Second Section - Struggles of Transmuation

One of the greatest struggles of transmutation is that as an alchemical discipline it often involves consideration of the natural laws of a singular plane, PM-1. There are some constants that are observed for the most part in other planes typically including reaction rates. Transmutation specialists when working as a field researcher on another plane often begin their study looking for familiar constants and then expanding from there. This is typically done by using well studied materials, isolates, or by conducting rate experiments. Some of the most common experiments for early data collection involve the alteration of certain metals and the rate of change observed.

Transmutation specialists also assist in understanding extraplanar materials and how they might relate to PM-1 materials. For instance, many materials of EXT-918 hold similar characteristics to materials of PM-1 that have similar names. Transmutation specialists are also very important for rate reduction when considering defense research. In short Transmutation specialists are highly interdisciplinary theorists whose study aims to create the previously unobtainable to better understand the laws that the universe is governed by and our relation to each other but they can also change the expected presence and function of metals and raw materials.
