Frames

Getting back into things I just had to refresh myself as to where we left off. It's time to talk more about frames.

What does a frame look like? To be honest it doesn't "look" like anything without some assistance but typically is seems like lines that support the structure like a wire skeleton of the construct in question. The frame can exist outside of the material body of the construct but it is highly recommended against as it could run into a material that could erase it such as lead. While not entirely necessary it is common practice to link all lines to create a full frame as it is often simply more practical but for full frames that need to be created quickly often multiple programmers will work to create segments of the total construct before linking them and making certain that the programming works together synergistically. The largest of constructs have used very large teams but with large numbers of people there is frequent errors in communication with the segments and often people will need to go through and repair degradation as the construct is built. This means giant wartime constructs aren't really practical though if you see a large full frame it is mostly to intimidate.

For this portion of talking about the building of the frame I'll try to guage your understanding and then build from there.
