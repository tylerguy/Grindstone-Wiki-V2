First Section - Early Education and Orders

BrunoGelehrte

Like your world the systems of education in our world took a very long time to develop and their history a very complicated web.  Even now there are several systems that can be considered educational institutions.  Beyond this every country seems to have it's own standards and practices. 

Perhaps the ideal place to begin is chronologically the earliest I can and work forward to the present.  The sapient materialfolk have been securing our own safety and advancement through the education of others since the most primitive technology.  We began through oral warning of dangers including where predators might be to what not to eat.  Not long after the earliest civilizations began to develop and primitive written word was conceptualized we began needing systems to learn how to transcribe text.  The earliest texts required years to master and this meant time that was not spent in labor so religious leaders and governors of communities often had access to education while others took to learning trades of their parents. 

This system suited the early people fine until societies became ever more complex and cultural integration became just as much a tactic territory control as warfare was.  The early temples were the first to adopt new systems of education forming monasteries that aided by providing increased income beyond tithe.  These isolated communities were the ideal place to develop ideas and early technology.  With the option to provide services outside of the products manufactured in these monasteries many temples wished to seize the opportunity for additional revenue but recognized the risk to the culture of monasteries by having those whom left, under the permission of those who governed in exchange for divine favor, their local lands for a new life and community.  This issue would soon be rectified with the advent of Castra Fidei.  These were places created to train artisans for hire but also house newly acquired resources for the temples under the protection of their own private forces. 

The earliest Orders were permitted to co-exist often but it did not take long until local governors gained increase concern for the military power growing in their own lands.  This was exacerbated during early land seizures in what is now the Central Territories for religious dominance.  It would be almost a century however until strict alternative systems would be put into place and before that the wealthy would hire religious scholars and other experts to be the educators of their young.  During the early Imperial Era of the Azalt Emperor Zephastus commanded the creation of a secular order for the education of aetherial use.  He feared the ever growing presence of religious Orders and commissioned a taxable and more controllable Order.  The newly established order was intended to be a political instrument and extension of the military while also being without a divine commitment. 

Unfortunately for the Emperor those concerned with the eternal in his court had him assassinated before a temporal Order could be established.  With the policies only partially established a deal conducted with a temple those in power felt they could trust everything seemed back in motion.  The Order of The Magus was established but in their creation their first leading entity the Arch Magus announced it's secularity in so far as separation from the temple of Belthid but with Belthidian ideologies and to exist as an apolitical entity.  It's believed that this decision is what led to a later ruler's support of some of the first religious reform acts.

Second Section - The Rise of the Academia

BrunoGelehrte

Near the end of the Imperial Era of the Azalt lived a man by the name of Academus whose land would be worked into a place of reflection shortly after his death.  Academus was a farmer and a man of little importance to history aside from what he left behind.  The place of reflection was crafted using monies earned by his son as an educator.  He referred to this place of study and reflection his Academia.  Legend has it, it's because that is where his father was buried.  While the name of this educator is lost to history his greatest student Tor would set in motion a new way of thinking and discovery. 

After it's establishment the cost of attending lecture at the Academia was simply the cost to sustain it and therefore many of those with the interest of further their education attended.  Due to its position in the world and the peaceful time many soldiers often found their way to the Academia.  The existing Emperor was fearful of another order being established close to where he ruled but also saw the Academia as a opportunity that could not be ignored.  He proclaimed Tor a brilliant mind and built upon the Academia to supply other minds, his students, access to resources for the betterment of his nation.  The Academia was a Order for the nation in many ways. The Academia did not last but a few years however but that is not relevant to this lecture.

Third Section - Education Under Othello

BrunoGelehrte

More than a century after the loss of Academia the Eastern Kingdom that would become the Kingdom of Othello began to establish itself.  Religious education transformed over the century to house a third option, the temple.  The temple itself became a place of local education for youths that were not acquired for the peoples tax or were too young at the time according to the local lord.  This is why many small communities often have only a few faiths they practice if not just one.  Religion and what faiths might practice were often restricted by local lords within the Old Kingdom.  The people's tax was established so the king could control the flow and training of artisans to help regulate power between the other lords with and without peerage.  The model allowed for the training of artisans by the nation, simple education to the youth, and aetherial education to still be regulated by The Order of the Magus which persisted all this time.  While it was illegal in the Old Kingdom to receive aetherial education outside the order illegal casters were often "overlooked" out of convenience.  It wasn't until after the revolution and the removal of the people's tax that new systems became necessary.

Some of you can probably guess where this is headed by now.

Fourth Section - The Genesis of UMAD

BrunoGelehrte

There were established places of local education in some of the largest cities that took on a secular stance.  These places educated the youth en mass for those who could afford it.  These paid schools were rare however but under new rule have become a hot topic for the courts.  When Dial was established it was part of their city plan to create facilities to educate by application and sometimes lottery.  Towering in the center however would be an establishment created for the advancement of military defense and public education not too dissimilar to the old model of Academia.  In fact it is from this that it would be given its name in concept.  This place would also be used as a public access for unrestricted education of aetherial studies to those who passed proper admissions examinations.  The concepts taught within the walls would not have religious restriction and would also not necessarily condemn the study of any one aetherial school.  Thus it was Universal in it's teachings of Magic and established itself in a system similar to the Academia and located within the walls of Dial.
