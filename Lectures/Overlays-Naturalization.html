Naturalization

Naturalization is the process of a subject that is extraplanar to the plane by which they exist on changing characteristics due to constant exposure. We will discuss what some of these seemingly malleable characteristics are and how little we know about why they change later. We will however discuss a little on what they are. First however I will reference some information from Aetherial Studies. As a reminder there is Summoning, Dismissal, and Translocation schools. All three of these schools of aetherial casting have one thing in common, heavy reliance on the familiarity with Planar Studies.

Summoning can best be thought of casting a large net and pulling something from one plane to another. Specificity causes more filters for what the net is trying to collect and can increase the complexity of the casting. Translocation occurs with forced overlays by aetherial exhaustion. The most relevant school however to the discussion of naturalization is Dismissal magic. When dismissed, the subject can be banished to a plane that it is naturalized to. Dismissal magic has many other uses like dismantling tethers but we will also not discuss tethers and their features at this time. While dismissal and naturalization have been well researched there is still much to learn about them as well. In time we hope you identify the weak points in our study and assist in research, especially those in Planar Studies.

It is uncertain how long naturalization takes exactly but the measured changes by currently investigated variables seem very minimal. For instance, a subject from the Shadow may only differ in a period of a few ticks from naturalization from an identical subject from a plane on ring 3 once relocated to PM-1. Note however that studies are limited due to the length of time it takes for a subject to naturalize is considerably lengthy. There are some subjects such as elementals or divine servants that differ wildly in their naturalization period. It should be noted forced naturalization due to continued exposure to a plane for a divine servant is generally both dangerous and may cause the subject harm or upset them.
