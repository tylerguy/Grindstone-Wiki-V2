First Section - Casting Equipment

Starting from the beginning there are a few tools you'll need which are a four-point, an object, some components, and a damn good understanding of what you are doing.  When you cast a spell it must be intended, that means spells aren't cast accidently.  Overlays are spontaneous but programing is tough. 

Since casting is intentional you need clear instructions for the process to complete and for something to be programmed correctly or you need to have the appropriate setup in your four-point.  The Four-Point is a book, I prefer leather bound, that has four corners covered in an ornate blue metal.  This material pretty easily holds and releases the aether, think of it like a super conductor for the aether.  These corners store a good amount of aether for specific use.  Their positionality and superconductor nature creates what we call the "weak area" which is a thin point between the plane you are on and the aetherial matrix.  We will come back to the weak point in a moment.  The Four-point can sometimes have an additional four corner set-up on the opposite cover.  These double-sided four points aren't common but typically are used if one has a lot of notes and spells within their four-point or if they are a common ritual caster. 

The inside cover of a four-point typically contains the Aetherius Simplicior which is a fancy damn chart to help you cheat a bit when you're first getting started or in a hurry. The pages are set up a little differently than a typical journal as the side margins are MUCH bigger and visible lines typically go top to bottom separating them.  This is true through about 2/3rds of the Four-Point. 

Many four-points have alternating parchment and then wax parchment for those who like to put their components on their cook when they cast.  The latter third of the Four-Point is without lines and is typically where people record ritual instructions, create illustrations, or really whatever they want.  The section between the margins of a four-point is used to actually write the spells with something called "spell-ink", real creative name.  The ink is like typical ink but with granules of something called diamond-dust.  If completely dry it'll really tear up your insides if inhaled.  The stuff is commonly accepted to apt tradesmen as a universal currency but is not a very common sale item to people who don't cast spells.

Second Section - Aeonization, Summation, and Components

ElhamSchreiber

Spells are classified by the rate and quantity of aether required to cast them.  Those that require a lot of aether are dangerous and those that require a lot to be programmed fast are considered difficult and dangerous.  We call this separation "degrees" and spells of varying degrees are only classified this way according to the minimum aether and rate needed to cast the spell.  Things can always be enhanced.  While there are specific and named spells that exist understand that casting is a study that has a lot of flexibility and there are infinitely many ways to use aether through the process of casting.

The beginning of the process requires the initial spell line.  The amount of aether required to create the intial spell line is incredibly little and is one of the first things taught when becoming a caster.  The minor charge of aether moves to the aeonized object and from the four-point and is directed to the weak area to create an opening, this is what we call Aeonization.

After Aeonization comes Summation which requires concentrated beams of an seemingly infinitesimal radii to move through long distances within the aetherial matrix to suddenly stop and amalgamate into a small charge.  Several beams create several charges which in turn create ripples within the Aetherial Matrix like dropping several small stones into a pond but in 5-dimensions.  These ripples come together creating wave interference which sometimes negates but other times amplifies the waves.  This is what Summation is.  Summation allows one to control the direction of the aether and move it towards the small hole created by the initial line as well as the rate it flows into the initial line.  The size of the waves, direction, rate, and size of the hole all come into consideration when determining the movement of aether for a spell.

For many spells the use of components are necessary.  While synthesis magic eventually hopes to make components the thing of the past it is much too complicated and impractical currently.  Instead we use the aether for what we can and components do a lot of the heavy lifting.  For instance, it's easier to spark oil to be set aflame than to create fire in space, although people like me can do that.  In that case, oil is a component.  Don't put oil on your book though, tip from me.  The whole process does require a start and end determined by the spell lines.  Nearly every spell used now in days has the same initializing sequence and same termination sequence much like software needs in your world.  All the stuff in between is your programming to get to your desired goal, BOOM.

This has been a real brief overview of things however.  There is lots of math and theory like up-theory.  Multiple lectures.
