Characteristics of Frames

Pauchik

Directionality, Rate, and Intensity are the three characteristics of frames. Unlike a river, faucet, or any other analogy using water that might be used for the flow of the aether in the aetherial matrix there is a command pulse for the interaction of the segments of a frame. Segments are portions of a line that have a terminus, sometimes two, which the aetherial pulse travels through and obeys certain simple actions. Some lines within the frame could have dozens, hundreds, or in extreme cases thousands of segments to properly program. In the case of the large number of segments many of the segments might have several directions of facing terminus' and this creates a visual that if you could look very, very closely the lines begin to look like a bunch little lines cluttered and intersecting. Directionality is complicated and not well understood but it is understood well enough with some general rules to create the programs. Directionality is directly linked to "Up-Theory" and generally it is important to determine the spatial direction of the terminus for each segment for proper function. The Rate is the amount of pulses over a certain time once properly activated within a segment or line. Rate is separated into two types: resting and active. A resting rate is the pulse at any time when a particular stimuli is not in effect while the other is when that stimuli is. For instance imagine if a construct responds to a command word, when the word is spoken the active rate is determined otherwise the resting rate is present. Since constructs require a constant and steady stream of aether, to keep a frame in tact there must be a constant rate. That's why a resting rate is necessary. Lastly intensity is simply the amount of aether within each pulse.

Undead

Pauchik

Undead are typically what people think of when they think of Necromancers and with good reason! Our title comes from the idea we animate dead and we certainly do! I don't know a single programming specialist who hasn't animated before. Undead are much easier the create than other constructs, we work with a lot of what is already there. With that said Undead break down physically pretty fast so it's always good to have a specialist at fixing up the dead around. Someone to keep them limber and slow down the decay. Alchemists and Necromancers go hand in hand when they specialize on this cheap and efficient way to create constructs. Undead however do not make good constructs for a common person as they require so much maintenance and slowly more programming will need to be added to them to keep them functioning. Eventually they become indistinguishable from Full Frame constructs. This form of programming however has several uses. Wake Necromancers are people who animate loved ones for a proper goodbye. It is not believed that this is really the person saying goodbye so it's mostly for the loved ones. Also often a Wake Necromancer will need to make programming to assist with making the construct close to how the deceased was remembered by relying on information provided to them.

Undead are generally animated often during training as well.

Full Frames

Full Frames are the most complicated constructs of all. They require very large and elaborate frames but they are good for the manipulation of any material in space and can have a large list of commands. For defense constructs of metal can be programmed that are trained in combat and other things. These constructs however are very expensive and time consuming to create and therefore a separate craftsman is typically responsible for creating the body treating each one as a work of art. It is very rare that these are commissioned except by the government or large organizations. With that said Dial has a few such constructs that assist in defense, I even helped with some of the programming! With nothing to work off of, only masters of the craft make them.

Scrolls

The final type of constructs I will discuss today are Contingent Single Casters also known as scrolls. These items are made for efficient use, those casters who are not yet experts or are even novices but need to utilize a spell outside their own ability. They have instructions to act through the whole spellcasting process but are not established in a way to repeat the process. These also require maintenance in the event they have aged too long or are too complicated. Generally they do what you expect, they cast a spell with little effort, once.

These contructs are small frame occupying little space but they can be very complicated. As you can see constructs come in all sorts of shapes and sizes and really it just takes some imagination and ingenuity.
