Literature

Literature is essentially a written (or in your case, printed) work that withstands the test of time, and is essential to its genre. There is no real reason to bother with the history of literature itself, as that is a complicated subject that involves a blend of cultural and religious histories over several eras. Instead, we'll be focusing on certain topics within literature and some examples of written works that are popularized within those.

Of course books are difficult to come by on PM-1 and are quite expensive and valuable, as each must be painstakingly transcribed by hand if you want to create a copy of an original work. Attention to detail is necessary to prevent translation errors, and not all concepts translate across language barriers. While it is true that some programming exists to transcribe written works using aetherial mechanisms, only insanely rich assholes do that. So there is not an easily accessible means of mass-production; nor is the literacy rate of most nations high enough to warrant a need for mass production.

Types of Literature

Many kinds of literary works exist, some for the sake of entertainment while others purely as a means of study and theorycrafting. 

As many of you who recently took the abridged REEEEE may recall, a certain play was mentioned, called Arabella's Fortune. It was written a number of years ago at the start of the Fur War between Victoria and the Blue Orc Territories, as a piece of entertainment media with politically charged themes at its center.

Through the course of the story, Arabella uses her position and power within society to undermine and demean or endanger the lives of others for her personal amusement, with no recognized consequence. It proved disastrous as a performance piece that left crowds angry and seeking some sort of catharsis or release - which was intentional by its author as a means of stirring up the hearts of the people against the aristocracy. The play was quickly banned as a public danger, and its author arrested for inciting riots and disturbing the peace. The work was taken and turned into a book however, which appears in many collections due to its power as a political piece.

There are popular works that exist as careful transcriptions of the journals of aetherial scholars or religious scholars of old, that document the growth of ideas and understanding of the natural laws of the universe, or the spreading and growth of various faiths.

A popular piece that relates to Planar studies are "Introduction to Interdimensions" which is considered a very concise and layman or sophomoric guide to the theory behind planes and their relations to each other, as well as how the principles of different schools of magic relate to planar position and movement of them within the aether.

Another more advanced piece of Planar studies is "The Sum of All Vertices" which attempts to accurately map out planar positions, and goes into depth on the theory and mathematics behind schools of magic like Dismissal and Summoning.

One of my particular favorites was not intended as a book, but was created as one nonetheless. It is called "Inhio" based off of the word covet/desire, and it is a series of poems and love letters exchanged between two people who were very much in love, but kept apart due to political marriages. Decades later, when both had passed, the estate of one of them was being sold off and the letters were found in a hidden compartment of an ornate desk.

Rather than simply disposing of them, the man who found the letters was moved by the words within them and had them compiled together, and transcribed into small books to share with others who might be moved by the letters. Both sides of the love affair were kept anonymous for the sake of avoiding scandal, and this added air of mystery made it a very popular piece that spread among young men and women.

Forbidden Texts

To wrap things up before final questions, there are of course... The forbidden texts. Not all information is or should be publicly available or even allowed within private collections. It is rare, but some works are considered so dangerous or controversial that knowing about them is considered enough to question your intent and character - and owning them is a serious offense. If the government of your nation may not necessarily punish you, the court of public opinion and social peers will essentially amount to a death sentence for your reputation and that of your family.
