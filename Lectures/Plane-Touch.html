Introduction

Due to complications finding another lecturer I will act as the lecturer for the Planar Research Department.

This lecture will cover the basics of Plane Touch which is a condition that is one of the many risks of planar travel. Plane Touch has several ways of manifesting but there are a few patterns to Plane Touch that have been observed. It is my hope to properly caution you to this condition. I will start by saying that I am read on the topic but my level of expertise is not identical to some members of the Planar Research Department.

Plane Touch

Plane Touch is generally characterized as an alteration of your mental state that occurs after travel to another plane of existence and does not leave even after return to a native plane. Frequency of travel and the number of planes one has traveled directly influence the chances of plane touch occurring. The Plane Touch condition has various levels of intensity and several different ways it can manifest, the most minor of which might not be immediately apparent. It is known however that the condition becomes exponentially more dangerous to the individual and alters their perception in a greater way as more travel occurs once the condition has manifested. There is no way to determine what trip might cause the condition until after it has happened and some people are able to travel more often than others before the condition manifests. Generally the most mild of cases present as anxiety and slight paranoia. The most extreme cases lead to rationalizing the unthinkable, very aggressive activity, sensory shutdown, multi-sensory hallucinations, and eventually parts of your body failing or atrophying. Cross examining the research of your plane it is best described as a disconnect from the nervous system. This could mean one "forgets" how to breath or experiences paralysis. What is most troubling is the condition can occur with as little as a single movement from native plane to a foreign one.

Plane Touch has numerical classifications to determine intensity. Class 1 means the condition is present but not outwardly apparent even without alchemical treatment. This class is very difficult to determine its presence without intensive investigation and may even slip by without the afflicted knowing. Class 2 is outwardly apparent without alchemical treatment but allows the afflicted to function uninhibited. Class 3 requires the use of alchemical treatment to function as though not afflicted. Class 4 means dependency on others is required without alchemical treatment and their life is greatly affected even with treatment. Class 5 is potentially lethal without treatment and dependency is created even with treatment. It is not certain why but Class 4 will spontaneously worsen either suddenly or by slow degradation into Class 5. Due to the limitations of treatment Class 4 is regarded as terminal.

My apologies as I'm pacing myself. This is a sensitive subject for me. While I have never been determined to be Plane Touched I've seen a lot of my friends go through it. I am one of the lucky ones who has never been afflicted.

The condition of Plane Touch even in Class one is the beginning of a very fast downward slope should there be continued travel. While there are recorded instances where the individual who was Plane Touched did not experience their condition getting worse after travel it is very rare. Typically additional travel causes extreme changes to the condition. Some who are afflicted Class 1 may jump to Class 4 after a single jump to another plane. During field exercises off plane there is an expectation of loss so they are routinely over staffed. Those who typically take on these assignments are provided compensation for the hazards of Planar travel. With this noted it is also understood that once one has traveled the stress or whatever it might be that causes this condition will not occur again should an individual travel to the same plane. For this reason those who have traveled off plane will often be reassigned to duties on the same foreign plane as they may travel in a relatively safe way from then on.

Even with the safety from further deterioration however travel to other planes comes with additional risks. Sometimes ones own physiology takes additional stress from the travel as the Belthidian laws differ from one plane to another. It is important to recognize that all planar travel comes with risks and only those who are aware of and accept these risks should do field work off plane. While we do need researchers to conduct extra-planar work the Planar Research Department has plenty of on-plane work as well. Please make certain in the future that this work is something you wish to be part of when you ask for extra planar assignments in the future should you become part of our research team.
