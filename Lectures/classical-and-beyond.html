Classical Casting

The Classical Era of Aetherial Efferent Casting is very similar to the current process of New Mastery. An aeonized object and four-point are utilized with advanced instructions and often pre-programmed spell aids are utilized in the four-point. The Classical Era lasted up until Enchanter Laster Thorpe of Parne conceived of the three steps to casting and theories of charge for the aether describing the aether as not one fluid substance but a wild slurry of three charged packets.

Before Enchanter Thorpe and after Caeso there was a time of innovation and cunning. While full systems of spells could not easily be printed most of a spell was present during the Classical Era. These partitioned spells were unpoetically named Chunk Spells which had gaps and required advance knowledge still in mathematics and current theory to parse together the chunks. Expert casters would often try to event new chunks or try to close the gap between current existing chunks but vital information and patterns had yet to be discovered. While theory and Toristic Process were strong during this era it became an assumption there were some objects that were superior to others for casting and particular shapes were the most important. This led to something called a pointer. Chunk casters were often very slow at casting but their casting safer and more specific as they also utilized very small aeonized areas. The chunk and pointer casters soon became called Wenders for how slow they were which is where we derive the word wand from. The Wender caster casts from the Wand.

Wenders took their object very seriously and due to the limitations on casters would often make them a noticeable feature for themselves. After the settlement of the North Eastern Kingdom and its growth leading to the exile of what are now colloquial called Mountainfolk the political game involving aetherial efferent casters did not end. Due to the need for secrecy more practical objects became popular for both criminal casters and the Order of the Magus. While Wenders were dying out Chunks became bigger and bigger. Aeonization and Programming became more well understood but the research of new spells was a tiresome process often taking very long periods of intense concentration because it was never certain what the patterns might look like at a given moment. This led to critical analysis of aetherial wave patterns across large area from the Order of the Magus. The data collected over decades would have been a waste if not for an agreement between early Westerners and a less than spiritual Red Cloak. The leak of information however combined with the spiritual advise of tontured Arch Clerics led Lord Magnus Beaufort, a mathematician and man of nobility of the time, to surmise general rules for the study of the aetherial matrix and its wave patterns.
