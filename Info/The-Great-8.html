Lay'Ora

“The Chained One”

Goddess of

  • Absence
  • Finality
  • Destruction
  • Painlessness
  • Function of Mortality

Holidays

  • February 4th
  • Dial New Year’s Eve

Notes

  • One of two Great 8 Gods to be gendered
  • At one time, was one of the largest faiths but now might be the most fragmented.

History

  • In Belthas, well known as “The Chained One”, due to the narrative of her punishment after defiance of the new laws post-first ascension
  • Worship was illegal during the era of Othello due to social backlash towards the faith and in turn, the superstitions that developed.
  • feelings of animosity towards Lay'Oran worship come from post Azalt ideologies.

Symbols

  • holds one of the oldest holy symbols
    • first symbols were very simple in shape, hers was simply a filled in circle.
  • new symbols were adopted and now there are several

Worship/Beliefs

  • Uniquely have no perpetuity
    • Worshippers understand their consciousness is lost as they join their goddess in a one-ness.
      • argued that no faith is closer to their god in death but some argue that without this consciousness no one truly is with her
  • Typically cremate though was not always the case
    • do not permit their body to be animated or investigated for resurrection
  • It is accepted that there are limitations and flaws with the practice of their faith due to needs of the body and society.
    • New places of worship or community infrastructure enable the spread of the domains of their god and even celebrate the completion of structures
      • recognize that this, in a way, is antithetical and practice spiritual penance for the healing of the soul and mind regularly afterwards
      • The creation of temples is necessary but not holy
  • Has a simple altar for quick construction
    • Emergencies
    • Small organized prayer
    • Last rights
  • Southern Tradition
    • Have their own funeral rights
      • Worshippers often have a place in the funerals of most people and act as one of the speakers
    • Differs from West of the mountains in North Central Territories where they have the most spare ceremonies for their dead
      • Considers it more a hygienic and right act to cremate
  • Spreading Domains
    • Worshippers hope to release her from the restraints she’s been punished with by outgrowing them.
      • No hope for cataclysmic event or revenge but instead seen as a moment of reunion with her
  • Often believe all Gods have the same goal
    • Accepting there is an eventuality and an end to all things
      • Some believe it’s their holy duty to hasten the process though also accept that their duty is the spreading of domains and not divine conclusion.

Texts

  • Lay’Ora is referenced in several, the primary holy texts are borrowed from texts of Belthid.
  • Several old accounts
    • Lost during Azalt times
    • Reference parables, recipes for foodstuffs consumed at certain times of the year and ceremonies
