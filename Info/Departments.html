Aetherial Studies

Aetherial Studies is a research department dedicated to the study of movement of aether that exists as the matrix for which all planes exist within. They also study spellcraft and the theory behind it including its three component parts being aeonization, summation, and programming. Aeonization is the process by which an opening is created between the plane of interest, the one being cast on, and the aetherial matrix that usually requires an aeonized object or spell book with four point casting. Summation is the manipulation of wave patterns within the Aetherial matrix to move aether to the opening in the plane from aeonization and helps regulate the rate of flow of aether. Programming is not to be confused with the programming school and is the process of converting aether into something else, this is where you determine what the spell being cast is. Some spells require more aether while others a greater rate of flow for aether. One should only summate what they are prepared to program. The schools of magic are also defined by our experts within this department. - Blaise, pinned message on the Aetherial Studies interdepartmental channel

TL;DR: Aetherial Studies can be boiled down to "The study of magic and how to use it", and spellcasting is a major part of the lives of the researchers at UMAD and the plane in which they reside.

Alchemy

Within this department alchemical research is conducted.  Belthidian law is considered through Toristic processes of observation not too dissimilar to the scientific method.  Concepts of the physical and metaphysical are described, applied, and further evaluated through the utilization of elementary alchemical components and use of equipment.  This research is important not only for the creation of new products in several industries but allows for closer examination of the Belthidian laws on the material within the world.  Proper tools, resources, and planar positional  are all considered when examining these laws closely. - Blaise, pinned message on the Alchemy interdepartmental channel

TL;DR: Alchemy is the closest thing to pure science in UMAD's plane. Belthidian Law is their equivalent of the laws of physics and the Toristic Process is their version of the scientific method. In this department, what we would call physics, chemistry, and biology are brought under the umbrella of Alchemy.

Cultural Studies

Cultural Studies, often simply called Culture, is currently one of the largest departments within UMAD due to the number of subjects examined here.  The study of history, communications, religion, economics, politics, and many others are found within this department.  While there has been consideration of separating this department in the future all these studies remain within a single department as it is the goal of the department to understand the interactions, motivations, and behavior of people.  Both social research and experimentation is conducted here. - Blaise, pinned message on the Cultural Studies interdepartmental channel

TL;DR: The Culture department deals with a wide range of subjects that can best be classified as "constructs of civilisation". If something has evolved as part of the advent of society, it'll likely fall under Culture.

Engineering

The Engineering Department is the most frequently utilized for military research and development but it also is responsible for several civil projects especially during reconstruction.  The engineering department studies the Belthidian Laws, their effects on material , and the utilization of these ideas to design and improve machines of function or processes.  Please note this department presently lacks a Department Head. - Blaise, pinned message on the Engineering interdepartmental channel

TL;DR: The Engineering department seems very similar to our understanding of engineering - applying science (or in their case, Belthidian Laws) for construction and the advancement of technology.

Necromancy

The Necromancy Department contrary to the understanding most have is not limited to the animation of dead tissue.  While service undead are produced by employees here the research team is dedicated to the advanced study of Aetherial programming.  They understand in collaboration with other departments how to create immaterial and Aetherial systems that interact with material.  Constructs of glass, metal, wood, or any real material are programmed by designing complicated multidimensional programs to cover an area called an Aetherial frame.  Aside from constructs and animation the necromancy department is responsible for stationary constructs often referred to as enchanted objects. The Necromancy department assists in security, locating and correcting issues within programming, and the theory of programming the aforementioned constructs. - Blaise, pinned message on the Necromancy interdepartmental channel

TL;DR: Necromancy both deals with the matters of undead and making magical constructs.

Planar Studies

Planar Studies, typically referred to as Planar, examines extraplanar interplay.  This department observes the Belthidian Laws and how their interactions with material differ within five-dimensional loci and the interaction between other planes in relative space.  This is typically conducted through the mass summation of aether to produce planar overlay and observing the change in natural phenomena.  This description requires adequate warning as determined by The Cultural Research Department as the study of extra-planar space comes with several risks.  While the laws on some planes may change the interactions with material causing one to quickly re-evaluate the definition of “air” others could cause the loss of the concept of time or alter personal perception to be incompatible with their native plane.  Many Planar Studies Researchers act as field researchers and conduct inter-departmental studies to further our understanding of the universe beyond their own plane.  The examination of how space in the universe affects the reality we live in has nearly limitless applications. - Blaise, pinned message on the Planar Studies interdepartmental channel

TL;DR: Planar Studies deals with all matters to do with the layout of the planes, planar travel etc. Planar travel while very useful can have a lot of harmful effects, the most notable of which being plane-touch.

Notes on Planar Studies

The universe exists in 5 dimensional space and so requires two models to visualize it.  The biconical model and the fluid disk model. The fluid disk is a fluid mosaic of amorphous ever changing shapes that have a general region defined proximally to the center of all things or from the point of the reality that people are familiar with.  Think of it like a flat disk-like surface with small areas that are constantly in flux as to where their walls are. The space between them is not empty, it’s called aether which moves fluidly and can be disrupted. The space the aether doesn’t occupy is filled by the limits of these planes of existence. When a clear opening exists between two realities they can overlap at points.

This is called a planar overlay and strange things can happen at these points. The wave patterns of the aether can be disrupted through a process called summation which is the second step involved in aetherial magic casting of three. Summation is essentially the summation of wave patterns through small manipulations that accumulate.  In addition to this design is the idea of rings.  Rings are concentric circles radiating out from the center of reality; however by tradition the first ring is defined as where the plane that the living are familiar with is located, the center of all things is the eighth ring, and the side of the rings opposite the living reality continues to ascend. So while planes may be the same distance from the center as the living reality (this term has been retired) one is the 15th ring and the other is the 1st ring.  Space may be interacted with in a 5 dimensional manner however meaning there are additional dimensions within these “flat surfaces” expressed in the fluid disk model. The biconical model was therefore developed to express this and time dilations in space.  In the biconical model the universe looks like an hourglass where the central point is the center of reality much like the center of the disk was too but it also shifts with overlays to explain the passage of time. Time moves in one direction, there is no going back in time even for gods.  The rate of passage is determined by proximity to the center however meaning the perception of time slows as you get closer to the center. 100 years might pass in one place in reality before 1 year elsewhere.

Addendum: These models are still used today but ideas within these notes are contested.
